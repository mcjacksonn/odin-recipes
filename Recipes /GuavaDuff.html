Guava Duff

Taste the Islands

This pudding duff is a traditional favorite of the Bahamas, where guavas and tasty rum sauce collide

Ingredients

  • Fresh guavas
  • Sugar
  • Cinnamon
  • Flour
  • Baking Powder
  • Salt
  • Shortening
  • Milk
  • Egg
  • Butter
  • Icing Sugar
  • Boiling Water
  • Dash of Salt
  • rum

Steps taken from (justapinch.com)

  1. Guava Duff Directions:
    1. Peel guavas, cut in half and remove seeds. Dice the fruit and strain to remove juice. Save juice to flavor sauce if desired.
    2. Put fruit in saucepan with water to cover; add sugar, cinnamon and allspice. Simmer until fruit is soft.
    3. Combine flour, baking powder and salt. Cut in shortening. Stir in milk and egg to form a soft dough.
    4. Knead until smooth. Roll out like a jelly roll on a floured board. Place the guava pieces on the center of the dough and roll over until the dough is spiraled with guava.
    5. Seal edges carefully. Wrap dough in a cotton or linen bag, or foil and parchment paper, tie the top securely and put into a large pot of boiling water for 1 hour or more if necessary to set the duff.
    6. Cream butter until soft but not melted.
    7. Beat confectioners sugar in gradually. Add boiling water, salt and rum or brandy.
    8. Beat until smooth and fluffy.
